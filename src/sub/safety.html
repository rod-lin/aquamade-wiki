Differences between biosafety level

BSL1

Mechanical pipetting only (no mouth pipetting allowed)
Safe sharps handling
Avoidance of splashes or aerosols
aily decontamination of all work surfaces when work is complete
Hand washing
Prohibition of food, drinks and smoking materials in lab setting
Personal protective equipment, such as; eye protection, gloves and a lab coat or gown
Biohazard signs
Using universal microbes: E.coli to extract enzymes.

BSL2

Eye protection and face shields can also be worn, as needed: wearing gas mask when preforming experiment related to NO and NO2
The laboratory has self-closing, lockable doors.
A sink and eyewash station should be readily available.
Biohazard warning signs: note the poisonous chemical compound: methyl vionlogen, placing this compound elsewhere.
